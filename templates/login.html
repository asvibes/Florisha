Did you know?

"The oldest living tree on Earth is a bristlecone pine over 5,000 years old — it was already ancient when the pyramids were built."

— Dendrochronology records, White Mountains, California

"Bamboo is the fastest-growing plant on the planet, capable of growing up to 91 cm in a single day under the right conditions."

— Guinness World Records, Phyllostachys edulis

"A single oak tree can support over 500 species of insects, birds, and fungi — making it one of the most biodiverse habitats on land."

— Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew

"The Rafflesia arnoldii produces the world's largest individual flower — up to 1 metre across — and smells of rotting flesh to attract pollinators."

— Botanical Society of the British Isles

"Trees in a forest communicate and share nutrients through an underground fungal network — often called the 'Wood Wide Web'."

— Dr. Suzanne Simard, University of British Columbia
